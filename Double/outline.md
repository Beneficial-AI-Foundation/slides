# Title: Double Categories (Categories Internal to Cat)

# Internal Categories
* The idea: what if objects and morphisms are themselves categories?
* Internal category in a category A with pullbacks: C0, C1, source, target, identity, composition
* The pullback for composition captures composable pairs
* Category axioms internalized as commutative diagrams
* Category internal to Set = small category

# Double Categories
* Category internal to Cat gives a double category
* The four components: objects, vertical morphisms, horizontal morphisms, 2-cells
* Visualizing a 2-cell as a square with four boundaries
* Vertical composition: stacking 2-cells
* Horizontal composition: placing 2-cells side by side
* The interchange law ensures compatibility

# Examples of Double Categories
* Squares in a category: simplest double category
* Spans: horizontal morphisms are spans, composed via pullbacks
* Profunctors: objects are categories, horizontal are profunctors, vertical are functors

# Why Double Categories?
* 2-categories have only one kind of 1-morphism; double categories have two
* Models situations with two different "directions"

# Monads in Double Categories
* Monads in 2-categories: endomorphism with unit and multiplication
* Horizontal monads in double categories
* Key insight: categories are monads in Span(Set)
* Why double categories beat bicategories: lax monad morphisms = functors

# Bimodules
* Bimodules are morphisms between monads on different objects
* (S,T)-bimodule: horizontal morphism with left and right actions
* Bimodules in Span = profunctors
* The double category of monads: Mnd(Span) ≃ Prof
* Bimodules are the natural 1-cells between monads
